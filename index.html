PM Thinking in Action

Case Study: From Invisible Failure to Automated System

A critical operational problem nobody had named - how I identified it, built the business case, and scoped the fix before it became a go-live incident.

Product
Enterprise GenAI Platform
My Role
Embedded Product Manager
Problem Type
Operational Risk / Zero Observability
Outcome
Manual chain eliminated
✕ Before
✗9-step fully manual provisioning chain with no documentation
✗Zero visibility into provisioning status for users
✗No error state, no retry mechanism, no alerting
✗Failures discovered by users manually checking SharePoint
✗No owner, no SLA, workaround was a Slack ping to admin
✓ After
✓Automated trigger replaces all 9 manual steps on project creation
✓Real-time provisioning status visible to users in the platform
✓Automatic error detection with retry logic and failure alerts
✓Admin monitoring dashboard with full provisioning visibility
✓System owns the process - defined owner, defined SLA, zero manual retries
The PM Lesson

Engineering had flagged this problem before. Nobody acted. The difference was reframing it from a technical debt item into a business risk with a cost - consultant downtime, project delays, go-live exposure. The same fix, with a business case attached, got stakeholder sign-off immediately. Product management is often about finding the frame that makes the right thing obvious.
